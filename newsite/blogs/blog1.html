Planning a Trip to Bahrain? Here’s What You Should Know!
With the right guidance and information, planning a trip to Bahrain could be a breeze for you. There are so many things to do that if you are not aware of beforehand, you might end up getting confused. In this article, we are going to cover the type of accommodation options, general information, and the must-visit places so that you can have a memorable trip without investing too much time in the research.
Accommodation Options
Bahrain is a popular tourist destination with a variety of accommodation options to fit a variety of budgets and preferences. The following are some of the most common types of accommodation in Bahrain.
Luxury Accommodation
Bahrain is home to some of the most luxurious hotels and resorts in the world. These properties provide top-tier amenities and services to meet the requirements of the most sophisticated visitors. Four Seasons Hotel Bahrain Bay, The Gulf Hotel Bahrain, Ibis Seef Manama, and Sofitel Bahrain Zallaq Thalassa Sea & Spa are among the most famous luxury hotels in Bahrain.
Budget-Friendly Accommodation
If you're searching for a more affordable option, Bahrain has plenty of budget-friendly places to stay. Budget travelers who want to save money on accommodation should consider staying in hostels or guesthouses.
Serviced Apartments
Serviced apartments are a common choice for travelers who prefer a more private and comfortable stay. These apartments provide all of the amenities and services of a hotel while providing privacy. They are ideal for traveling with families, couples, or groups of friends.
Boutique Hotels
Boutique hotels are becoming increasingly famous in Bahrain. These unique hotels provide a more personalized experience and are ideal for travelers seeking a more intimate stay. Boutique hotels in Bahrain are frequently housed in historic buildings and provide a blend of contemporary conveniences and traditional charm.
Must-See Places in Bahrain
Bahrain is a tourist location with a wide range of attractions for visitors of all types. Bahrain has something for everyone, from historical sites to contemporary entertainment centers. Here are some of the top-rated sites in Bahrain that you must add to your itinerary.
Bahrain National Museum
The museum features an impressive collection of artifacts depicting the country's history. Everything from ancient pottery to traditional attire, jewelry, and musical instruments can be found here. The exhibitions at the museum are well-curated and offer a fascinating glimpse into Bahrain's history.
Bahrain Fort (Qal'at al-Bahrain)
This UNESCO World Heritage site is one of the most important historical monuments in Bahrain. The fort was built in the 14th century and is a stunning example of Islamic construction. You can walk through the ruins of the fort and imagine what life was like for the people who once resided there. The view from the fort's vantage point is breathtaking, providing a panoramic view of the surrounding region.
Al Fateh Grand Mosque
The grand mosque is one of the world's largest mosques and a must-see sight in Bahrain. The mosque's breathtaking building and intricate design will take your breath away. Take a guided walk to the mosque to learn about Islam and Bahrain's cultural heritage. Visitors of all religions are welcome at the mosque.
Manama Souq
Manama Souq is a traditional market in the center of Manama, Bahrain's capital city. It's a lively and bustling place that gives visitors a taste of Bahrain's rich cultural heritage and past. The market is a labyrinth of narrow alleys and lanes lined with stores and stalls offering a variety of products such as textiles, jewelry, spices, perfumes, and handicrafts. Traditional Arabian apparel, souvenirs, and local delicacies are also available to visitors. Local artists can also be seen practicing traditional Arabian crafts such as weaving and pottery. The Bab Al Bahrain building, which was built in 1949 and functioned as Manama's gateway, is one of the market's most famous attractions.
Budget-Friendly Accommodation
If you're searching for a more affordable option, Bahrain has plenty of budget-friendly places to stay. Budget travelers who want to save money on accommodation should consider staying in hostels or guesthouses.
Al Dar Islands
Al Dar Islands are a group of four small islands off the coast of Bahrain, only a brief boat journey from the mainland. These islands are famous tourist and local destinations, with a variety of activities and attractions for visitors of all ages. The islands have several pristine beaches with crystal-clear waters that are perfect for swimming, snorkeling, and other water sports. Visitors can also enjoy sunbathing on the sandy beaches or discovering the natural beauty and wildlife of the islands. Al Dar Islands is a must-see for anyone visiting Bahrain, providing a one-of-a-kind and unforgettable experience of natural beauty, relaxation, and excitement.
The Tree of Life
The Tree of Life, also known as Shajarat-al-Hayat, is a natural wonder in the center of Bahrain's desert and truly a miracle. Despite the harsh desert conditions, this tree is believed to be over 400 years old and stands tall at around 9.75 meters. The Tree of Life is a symbol of resilience and endurance, having survived for millennia in a barren and arid environment. Its precise age and the source of its water supply are unknown, adding to its mystique and allure. The Tree of Life has cultural and spiritual importance for the people of Bahrain, in addition to its natural beauty.
Bahrain International Circuit
Bahrain was the first country in the region to hold Formula One in 2004. From the duel in the desert in 2014 to the thrilling opener in 2021, Bahrain has consistently provided a spectacle under the desert night sky over the last 17 years.
Other Information
Visa Requirements
Visitors from over 100 countries (including India) are eligible for a visa on arrival in Bahrain, while residents of a few countries can enter visa-free. The visa on arrival is good for up to 14 days and is extendable for a fee.
Weather Information
Bahrain has a hot and arid environment with very little rain throughout the year. Summer temperatures can hit 45°C, while winter temperatures range from 15°C to 25°C. The winter months are ideal for visiting Bahrain because the weather is mild and the humidity is low.
Flight Information
Bahrain is well-connected to major Indian cities, and several airlines operate direct flights between India and Bahrain. Some of the popular airlines that fly this route include Gulf Air, Air India, and IndiGo.
Bahrain offers a variety of accommodation options to fit any budget or preference, including luxury hotels, budget-friendly places, serviced apartments, and boutique hotels. The country has plenty of must-see places that are suitable for everyone. We hope that you have an unforgettable trip to Bahrain!
